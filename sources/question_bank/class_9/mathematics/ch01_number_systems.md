# Class 9 Mathematics — Chapter 1: Number Systems (outline for planning)

## Core ideas

- Real numbers and the number line
- Rational and irrational numbers
- Representing recurring decimals as rational numbers
- Laws of exponents for real numbers
- Square roots and cube roots of positive integers

## Skills

- Classify numbers as rational or irrational with justification
- Locate numbers on a number line
- Simplify expressions using exponent laws
- Convert recurring decimals to p/q form (simple cases)

## Contexts

- Measuring lengths that are not perfect squares
- Comparing quantities in science class
- Currency and measurement in daily life

## Misconceptions

- Treating π or √2 as rational because they appear in formulas
- Confusing terminating decimals with rational numbers only
- Applying exponent laws to addition instead of multiplication

## NCERT-style prompts to inspire (not copy blindly)

- Show that 0.̄3 can be written as 1/3 using algebra.
- Between any two distinct rational numbers, find another rational number.
- Simplify (2^(1/3)) · (2^(1/3)) using laws of exponents.
